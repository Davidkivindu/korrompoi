Dr. Rachel Korrompoi

Mental Health Consultant | Life Coach | Author | African Women’s Advocate

Dr. Rachel Korrompoi is a mental health consultant, certified life coach, and acclaimed author from Kajiado, Kenya. Her mission is to heal, equip, and empower African women and professionals to rise above trauma, reclaim their voices, and live with clarity and purpose.

Korrompoi's Biography

Dr. Rachel Korrompoi, Rooted in personal healing and cultural resilience, her journey has been one of breaking generational cycles of silence, mental suffering, and disempowerment. Since founding her mental health consultancy in 2020, Dr. Rachel has directly impacted over 1,000 women and youth through leadership and life coaching, mental wellness education, and financial empowerment programs.

She has worked extensively with grassroots groups and professionals alike—including SACCOs like Ollin Sacco and Ilkisongo Sacco, churches, and women’s associations—to champion mental health awareness and self-leadership strategies.

work boldly confronts taboo subjects such as gender-based trauma, FGM, cultural oppression, and emotional neglect, making her a courageous and necessary voice in the African wellness and empowerment space. Through her groundbreaking books—Healing of the African Woman, Rising Above Fear, Woman Reborn, The Resilient Mind, and Naked—Dr. Rachel offers hope and healing to women navigating complex personal and societal battles.

Despite societal barriers and political resistance, she stepped into the public arena, running for office and emerging as a symbol of courage for women in leadership. Her boldness and impact have earned her prestigious recognitions, including the Dependable Brands Award of Resilience and the Sound Mind Award for Mental Health Research and Leadership.

Mental Health Tips for a Stronger, Balanced Life

1. Start Your Day with Intention

Take 5–10 minutes every morning to breathe deeply, stretch, and set your intention for the day. A calm mind at sunrise helps carry peace into your day.

2. Protect Your Energy

Learn to say no to toxic conversations, overcommitting, or environments that drain you. Your peace is your power—guard it.

3. Talk About It

Don’t suffer in silence. Speak to a trusted friend, mentor, or mental health professional. Sharing lightens the load and opens doors to healing.

4. Disconnect to Reconnect

Take regular digital breaks. Step away from your phone, TV, or laptop to reconnect with yourself. Quiet time helps you hear your inner wisdom.

5. Move Your Body

Whether it's a walk, dance, or simple stretching—movement releases endorphins and reduces stress. Motion shifts emotion.

6. Stay Hydrated and Nourished

Food and water affect your brain chemistry. Choose balance over busyness. Eat to fuel your healing—not your hustle.

7. Journal Your Thoughts

Write down your feelings and experiences daily or weekly. Your journal is a mirror for your soul.

8. Sleep is Sacred

Prioritize rest. Your body and mind repair through quality sleep. Rest is not a reward—it’s a requirement.

9. Celebrate Small Wins

Acknowledge every step forward, even the tiniest ones. Progress, not perfection, is the goal.

10. Seek Help Without Shame

Therapy and coaching are tools of strength, not weakness. Asking for help is a brave act of self-love.

11. Set and Respect Your Boundaries

Know your limits—emotionally, mentally, physically, and spiritually. Say “no” without guilt and “yes” with intention. Boundaries are not walls—they are bridges to healthier relationships and inner peace. There is grace to saying No.

Empowering You to Manage Your Finances with Clarity and Confidence

- Welcome to your safe space for building financial peace of mind. Whether you're a woman navigating daily expenses, a business owner seeking stability, or someone recovering from financial burnout—these tips are crafted for you. Here’s how to take control, step by step:

1. Track Every Coin

- What you don’t track, you can’t transform. Keep a simple notebook or app to record all your income and expenses. Awareness is the first step to financial healing.

2. Create a Budget You Can Live With

- A budget is not punishment—it’s empowerment. Break your money down into needs, wants, savings, and giving. Start with simple monthly goals.

3. Save with a Purpose & a Community

Join a chama or SACCO where saving becomes easier and more accountable. Saving with others builds discipline and hope.

4. Set Real, Clear Goals

Want to pay school fees? Buy land? Start a business? Write it down, give it a deadline, and save toward it little by little.

5. Learn to Say “No” to Pressure

Financial boundaries are self-care. You don’t have to attend every fundraiser or lend money you don’t have. Protect your peace.

6. Avoid Unhealthy Debt

Only borrow to grow—not to survive. If the loan doesn’t generate value (business income, education, assets), rethink it.

7. Delay the Gratification

You don’t need to buy it now. Waiting 24–48 hours before a purchase helps you spend intentionally, not emotionally.

8. Change Your Money Mindset

You are not poor—you’re preparing. Speak life into your finances. Don’t say “I’m broke.” Say “I’m building.”

9. Teach Your Children Early

Start with small chores, rewards, and savings jars. Money habits begin at home. Raise financially wise children.

10. Ask for Help Without Shame

You’re not alone. Talk to a coach, elder, or group you trust. There’s no shame in financial struggle—only strength in learning and rising again.

11. Create Multiple Streams of Income

Relying on one income source is risky—especially in uncertain times. Start small: turn your skills, hobbies, or experience into side income. Whether it's farming, baking, selling online, or teaching, every extra stream brings freedom. When one source slows down, the others sustain you. Financial peace is built on diversity.

12. Learn a Skill or Trade

When you have a skill, you always have a way forward. Whether it’s tailoring, digital marketing, baking, hairdressing, farming, or public speaking—skills open doors to opportunity, dignity, and income.

Don’t wait for a job—equip yourself to create your own.
